Iceland

Land of Fire and Ice

About Iceland

Iceland's otherworldly landscapes have always fascinated me - it's like visiting another planet without leaving Earth. The land of fire and ice offers a unique combination of glaciers, volcanoes, geothermal hot springs, and lava fields that create a surreal environment.

What attracts me most is the raw, untamed nature and the opportunity to experience natural phenomena like the Northern Lights and the midnight sun that can't be found in most other places. Iceland's dramatic landscapes have been shaped by powerful forces of nature, making it a living showcase of geology in action. Despite its harsh landscapes, Iceland also boasts a rich cultural heritage with fascinating Norse mythology, Viking history, and a thriving contemporary arts scene.

Experiences I'm Dreaming Of

Witnessing the Northern Lights

Experience the magical Aurora Borealis dancing across the night sky in vibrant greens, purples, and blues. This natural light show, caused by solar particles interacting with the Earth's magnetic field, is one of nature's most spectacular displays and a bucket-list experience for any traveler.

Relaxing in the Blue Lagoon

Soak in the warm, mineral-rich waters of the Blue Lagoon geothermal spa, surrounded by a dramatic landscape of black lava fields. This iconic Icelandic experience offers relaxation and rejuvenation, with the milky blue waters known for their healing properties for the skin.

Exploring Ice Caves

Venture inside the heart of a glacier to explore the mesmerizing blue ice caves that form during winter. These temporary natural wonders create surreal landscapes with their translucent blue walls and ceiling formations, offering a rare glimpse into the interior of a glacier.

Feeling the Power of Gullfoss

Stand beside the massive Gullfoss waterfall and feel the raw power of nature as the Hvítá river rushes down in a spectacular two-tiered cascade. The mist rising from the falls creates rainbows on sunny days, adding to the magical atmosphere of this natural wonder.

Interesting Facts About Iceland

  • Iceland has no mosquitoes
  • It's one of the safest countries in the world, with one of the lowest crime rates
  • About 85% of Iceland's energy comes from renewable sources
  • Icelandic horses are unique - they have two extra gaits that horses elsewhere don't have
  • Icelanders believe in elves, and construction projects have been altered to avoid disturbing their habitats
  • The country has no standing army, navy, or air force
  • Iceland was the last place on earth to be settled by humans
  • The Icelandic phone book is listed by first name because last names are generally patronymic

Trip Planning

Best Time to Visit

June to August offers midnight sun and warmer temperatures for hiking and exploring. September to March is the best time for Northern Lights viewing. April to May and September to October are shoulder seasons with fewer tourists. Winter (November to February) is cold but magical with ice caves and snow-covered landscapes.

Must-Visit Areas

  • Golden Circle: Thingvellir National Park, Geysir geothermal area, and Gullfoss waterfall
  • South Coast: Black sand beaches, Skógafoss and Seljalandsfoss waterfalls
  • Reykjavik: Charming capital with unique architecture and vibrant cultural scene
  • Jökulsárlón: Stunning glacier lagoon with floating icebergs

Travel Tips

  • Pack layers regardless of season - weather changes quickly
  • Rent a 4x4 vehicle if traveling on Highland F-roads
  • Water in Iceland is pure and safe to drink straight from the tap
  • Bring a good camera for Northern Lights photography
  • Swimming pools are an important part of Icelandic culture - visit local ones
